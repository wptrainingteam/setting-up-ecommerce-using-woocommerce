Setting Up E-commerce Using WooCommerce

In this lesson you will learn the basics of using the WooCommerce plugin for WordPress.

In this Presentation, you will learn:

  1. Installation.
  2. General store settings.
  3. Adding and editing products.
  4. order management.
  5. Sales reporting.
  6. The system status report.

Log into your WordPress Dashboard and go to Plugins > Add New. In the search field, type “WooCommerce” and click Search Plugins.

Enter your store's location, currency accepted and the types of products you plan to sell.

Setup the payment methods used by your shop. You must register with your chosen payment method before you can accept payments using their services.

Set your shipping options. You can include weight and dimension units used to ship your products.

Your store is now setup and ready to add your first product! Click Create a product button to add a product to your store.

Adding a new product!

  1. Product categories and tags are similar to regular post categories and tags. Use product categories for grouping and product tags for details.
  2. You should use a category for you product and tags to list the different sizes and pricing. This means your picture and all its sizes will be listed as one item.
  3. Variable products are a product type in WooCommerce. They let you offer a set of variations on a product. You can control prices, stock, image, and more for each variation.
  4. Attributes are pieces of data. Use them to add more technical information to a product.

Go to WooCommerce > Products > Attributes section and add an attribute, for example "Size", click Add Attribute

Go to: Products > Add New. Adding a product feels like writing a regular post in WordPress, so the view of the form should be familiar.

Add a product image using the Product Image form (under product tags).

Select Variable product option in the Product Data dropdown list.

Here is a bit more detail about some of the other product options:

  1. A Simple product type describes the physical off-line product which requires shipping. A book or a laptop, which do not require variation, are simple products.
  2. A Virtual product is a product that doesn’t require shipping. For example, online tarot reading.
  3. A Downloadable product is similar to a virtual product. In this instance, the customer is given a file to download.

Open the Attributes section and select "Size"
